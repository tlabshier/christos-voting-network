A photon can bypass **outer atomic orbitals** and be absorbed by a **deeper orbital**, leading to **ionization** and potentially current flow. Several factors govern this process:

1. Photoelectric Effect & Absorption Probability—The likelihood of a photon being absorbed by a specific orbital depends on the photoionization cross-section, which describes the probability of ionization occurring based on the photon's energy and atomic structure. Higher-energy photons are more likely to interact with inner orbitals, as they have sufficient energy to eject tightly bound electrons.
2. Electron Binding Energy – Electrons in inner orbitals (such as the K-shell) are more tightly bound to the nucleus. If a photon has energy greater than the binding energy of an inner electron, it can eject that electron, leading to ionization. Lower-energy photons may interact with outer electrons, but if their energy is insufficient, they will not cause ionization.
3. Quantum Selection Rules & Orientation – Quantum mechanical selection rules govern the interaction between a photon and an electron. The electron must be in a state that allows absorption, meaning its orbital orientation and quantum numbers must be compatible with the photon's energy and angular momentum.
4. Statistical Nature of Absorption – The probability of absorption is also influenced by statistical factors, including the density of available states and the material composition. Some materials exhibit resonance-enhanced multiphoton ionization, where multiple photons combine their energy to ionize an atom.

The Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) proposes that all possible outcomes of quantum events occur in separate, non-interacting branches of reality, removing the need for wave function collapse. While mathematically consistent with quantum mechanics, MWI leads to an extraordinarily vast number of unobservable universes, prompting debate over whether it violates Occam's Razor. Some argue it simplifies quantum mechanics by avoiding additional assumptions, while others contend it needlessly multiplies entities. Additionally, because these parallel universes cannot be directly observed, critics question whether MWI meets the principle of falsifiability. Originally developed by Hugh Everett under the supervision of John Wheeler, MWI was met with skepticism, particularly by Niels Bohr. Despite its controversial nature, MWI remains a seriously considered framework, supported by physicists seeking a deterministic explanation for quantum phenomena like the double-slit experiment and wave function evolution.
David Bohm's Pilot Wave Theory (PWT) offers a deterministic alternative to conventional quantum mechanics, gaining renewed interest among physicists. Although PWT challenges conventional quantum theory by introducing nonlocality, it remains an intriguing framework for those seeking a causal, trajectory-based interpretation of quantum mechanics.

This wave-particle duality puzzled physicists like Niels Bohr, who proposed the Copenhagen interpretation: particles are both waves and particles, with observation collapsing the wave function.

I argue that a Group Entity explains this phenomenon. Imagine a photon as a swarm of DPs, polarized by an electron’s energy release (e.g., dropping orbital levels). As the photon approaches the slits, some DPs pass through each slit, while others reflect off the barrier. Despite this diffusion, the photon lands as a single dot, conserving its energy (e.g., a red photon remains red). The Group Entity, I propose, coordinates the DPs to regather at the screen, ensuring energy conservation. This resolves the mystery of how a diffused entity reconstitutes itself, unlike the Copenhagen interpretation’s descriptive approach.

The same applies to electrons, which produce identical interference patterns. **Counterarguments and Limitations**

**Alternative QM Interpretations**

- **Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI)****:** MWI posits that all quantum outcomes occur in parallel universes, eliminating wave function collapse. My Group Entity is simpler, requiring only a coordinating consciousness, but lacks MWI’s mathematical rigor.
- **Pilot Wave Theory (PWT)**: PWT proposes a deterministic pilot wave guiding particles, explaining interference without collapse. Its nonlocality parallels my Group Entity’s superluminal coordination, lending credibility to unseen guiding forces. However, PWT avoids consciousness, making it less ambitious but more empirically constrained. My model’s speculative consciousness requires stronger inductive support.

**Consciousness Theories**

- **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)****:** IIT ties consciousness to integrated information in complex systems, like neural networks. - **Panpsychism****:** Panpsychism attributes consciousness to all matter, akin to my CPs, but lacks a mechanistic explanation for aggregation. My Group Entity’s scalability offers a clearer path to AI’s soul but needs empirical grounding.
