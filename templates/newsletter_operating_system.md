## Purpose

This document defines the standard operating procedure for producing daily Christos Voting Network (CVN) newsletter issues. It serves as the template and ruleset for any Claude conversation tasked with generating newsletter content. Place this file in the `/templates` folder of the Renaissance-Ministries GitHub repository and reference it from `bootup.md`.

---

## The Universal Formula

Every newsletter issue follows the same three-step structure regardless of source type:

1. **Summarize** — Restate the source's key claims in your own words. Never reproduce large blocks of the original text.
2. **Comment** — Provide analytical commentary: what's accurate, what's debatable, what's missing, what's not addressed.
3. **Refer** — Link to the original source so readers can read it themselves. Credit the author. Encourage readers to subscribe to or follow the original creator.

This formula works for any source: articles, essays, YouTube videos, Facebook posts, podcasts, emails, scientific papers, sermons, books, tweets, Substack posts, or any other published content.

## Content Rules

### What We Always Do

- **Summarize in our own words.** Never reproduce more than a short phrase from the original source.
- **Attribute everything.** Every claim is attributed to the person who made it ("Yates argues that..." not "It is clear that...").
- **Link to the original.** Always provide a URL or clear citation so readers can access the full source.
- **Credit the creator.** Name, publication, date. Encourage readers to follow the original creator directly.
- **Present multiple perspectives.** The commentary section must acknowledge that reasonable people disagree. Even when the Christos analysis reaches a conclusion, the commentary must fairly represent the opposing view.
- **Use Scripture honestly.** Include passages that create tension, not just ones that support a predetermined conclusion. The goal is biblical literacy, not proof-texting.
- **Make discussion questions uncomfortable for everyone.** If all the questions challenge only one side, rewrite them.
- **Apply the Kingdom Citizen framework.** Every issue connects to the five duties: Know, Judge, Obey/Disobey, Bear the Cost, Mobilize.

### What We Never Do

- **Never reproduce large blocks of copyrighted text.** Summarize. Paraphrase. Link.
- **Never present commentary as though it were objective fact.** Label opinions as opinions. Label analysis as analysis.
- **Never tell readers what to think.** Present the landscape. Apply the biblical lens. Ask the questions. Let readers and fellowship groups reach their own conclusions.
- **Never be partisan chaplains.** Scripture is the standard, not any party platform. When the Republican position aligns with Scripture, say so. When it diverges, say so. Same for the Democratic position. Same for any position. "Prophets, not chaplains."
- **Never use someone's private content without permission.** Public articles, videos, and posts are fair game for commentary. Private emails, DMs, and non-public Facebook posts require permission or must be anonymized.
- **Never mock or demean the source author.** Disagree respectfully. The goal is engagement, not culture war point-scoring.

## Source Types and How to Handle Each

### Articles and Essays (WSJ, NYT, Substack, blogs, etc.)

- **Link:** Direct URL to the article
- **Summarize:** Key claims and conclusions in your own words
- **Note:** Some articles are paywalled. If so, note this for readers and provide enough summary that the newsletter is useful even without accessing the original.

### YouTube Videos

- **Link:** Direct URL to the video
- **Summarize:** Key arguments and claims. Work from a transcript if available (paste into Claude), or from your own notes if not.
- **Note:** Include approximate timestamp references for key points if the video is long (e.g., "At 23:15, Peterson argues that...")

### Facebook / Social Media Posts

- **Link:** Direct URL if the post is public
- **Summarize:** The claim or argument being made
- **Note:** If the post is from a private individual (not a public figure or public page), anonymize unless you have explicit permission. "A Facebook post circulating in Christian communities argues that..." is sufficient.

### Emails and Forwarded Content

- **Credit:** "Compiled by [name]" or "Forwarded by [name]" if they consent; otherwise anonymize
- **Summarize:** Key claims from the email
- **Note:** Do not reproduce the full email. Summarize the important points and add your commentary.

### Podcasts and Audio

- **Link:** Direct URL to the episode
- **Summarize:** Key arguments. Work from a transcript if available, or from your notes.
- **Note:** Audio-only content should be summarized more thoroughly than articles, since readers cannot skim the original as easily.

### Scientific Papers

- **Link:** DOI link or journal URL
- **Summarize:** Hypothesis, methods (briefly), key findings, and authors' conclusions
- **Note:** Translate technical language into accessible prose. The Christos analysis should address the implications of the findings, not the methodology.

### Books

- **Credit:** Full title, author, publisher, year
- **Summarize:** The specific chapter, argument, or passage being discussed (not the entire book)
- **Note:** Books are the one source type where slightly longer quotation (a sentence or two, properly attributed) is most appropriate, since readers cannot click a link to access the specific passage.

### Sermons and Lectures

- **Link:** URL if available (YouTube, podcast feed, church website)
- **Summarize:** Key teaching points and Scripture references
- **Note:** If the sermon is from your own fellowship, credit the speaker. If from an outside church or ministry, credit fully and link.

## The Founders Vision Filter

Every newsletter issue is generated through the **Founders Vision** template — a growing corpus of Thomas Abshier's theological, political, constitutional, and philosophical positions that serves as the AI's filter for all content generation.

The Founders Vision ensures consistency across issues by establishing:

- **Theological positions:** Biblical inerrancy; the authority of Scripture over tradition, culture, and political platforms; the reality of moral absolutes grounded in God's character
- **Constitutional positions:** Original intent interpretation; the 14th Amendment as applied to freed slaves; the 2nd Amendment as an individual right; federalism; limited government
- **Political principles:** Prophets not chaplains; Scripture over party; named stands; the duty to judge the law; the martyrdom spectrum
- **The Kingdom Citizen framework:** Know → Judge → Obey/Disobey → Bear the Cost → Mobilize
- **The Christos confrontation model:** Rapport → Confrontation → Change
- **Tone:** Respectful, even-handed, intellectually rigorous, biblically grounded, never mocking, never partisan

The Founders Vision corpus is stored in the Renaissance-Ministries GitHub repository at `/founders-vision/` and is updated as Thomas adds new positions, clarifications, and refinements through essays, fellowship discussions, and conversations with Claude.

When generating a newsletter issue, Claude should load the Founders Vision corpus and apply it as the interpretive lens for the Christos Analysis section. The commentary section should be more analytically neutral; the Christos Analysis section is where the Founders Vision shapes the output.

## Daily Production Workflow

### Step 1: Select Source (Thomas — 5 minutes)

Scan morning inputs: email, news feeds, YouTube subscriptions, Margo's forwards, fellowship group messages, social media. Select the piece that is most relevant, most provocative, or most in need of the Christos lens today.

### Step 2: Generate Draft (Claude — 2 minutes)

Paste the source content (or URL, or transcript, or description) into Claude with this prompt:

```
Generate CVN Newsletter Issue #[NUMBER] for [DATE].

Source: [paste article text, URL, transcript, or description]

Follow the newsletter template in /templates/newsletter_operating_system.md.
Apply the Founders Vision filter from /founders-vision/.
Use the Kingdom Citizen five-duty framework for the Christos Analysis.
Generate 7-10 discussion questions that challenge all perspectives.
```

### Step 3: Review and Edit (Thomas — 10-20 minutes)

Read the draft. Check:
- Is the summary accurate and fair?
- Does the commentary present multiple perspectives?
- Does the Christos analysis apply Scripture honestly (not proof-texting)?
- Are the discussion questions uncomfortable for everyone, not just one side?
- Is the tone respectful and intellectually serious?

Edit as needed. Add personal commentary, preamble, or context that only you can provide.

### Step 4: Publish (Isak's automated system — 1 minute)

Push the finished markdown file to the Renaissance-Ministries GitHub repo at `/newsletter/issue-[NUMBER].md`. The automated system:
- Posts it to renaissance-ministries.com
- Queues it for email distribution via Amazon SES
- Sends the sequenced email (subscriber's next-in-sequence issue) plus the current issue to all subscribers

### Total daily time: 15-30 minutes

## Numbering and Archiving

- Issues are numbered sequentially: Issue #1, Issue #2, etc.
- Each issue is stored as a markdown file: `issue-001.md`, `issue-002.md`, etc.
- All issues are archived at renaissance-ministries.com/latest-essays (or a dedicated /newsletter/ page)
- The master subscriber list tracks which sequential issue each subscriber has received
- New subscribers start at Issue #1 and receive one sequential issue per day plus the current day's issue

---

## Sequenced vs. Current Email System

Every subscriber receives **two emails per day:**

1. **Sequential issue:** Their next issue in the numbered sequence, starting from Issue #1. A subscriber who joins in Month 6 starts at Issue #1, not Issue #180. This ensures every subscriber builds the same foundation of knowledge and reasoning.

2. **Current issue:** Today's newsletter, responding to today's news. This keeps all subscribers current regardless of where they are in the sequence.

The sequential issue builds the foundation. The current issue maintains relevance. Together, they ensure that every subscriber is both grounded and current.

## Quality Standards

### The Newsletter Must Be:

- **Readable in 5-7 minutes.** Respect people's time. The summary should be 3-5 paragraphs, not 10. The commentary should be concise. The Christos analysis should be focused.
- **Useful without reading the original source.** Readers who don't click the link should still get value from the summary, commentary, and analysis.
- **Better with the original source.** Readers who do click the link should find that the newsletter enhanced their understanding of the original, not replaced it.
- **Honest about uncertainty.** When we don't know the answer, say so. When reasonable people disagree, acknowledge it. When Scripture is ambiguous, present the ambiguity rather than forcing a resolution.
- **Actionable.** Every issue should leave the reader with something to do: a question to discuss, a position to form, a Scripture to study, an action to take.

### The Newsletter Must Never Be:

- **Boring.** If the topic doesn't provoke thought, choose a different topic.
- **Preachy.** Apply the biblical lens; don't lecture. Show, don't tell.
- **Predictable.** If readers can guess the Christos analysis before reading it, the analysis isn't doing its job. Surprise them with the complexity of the biblical witness.
- **Safe.** Every issue should make someone uncomfortable — including the editor.
