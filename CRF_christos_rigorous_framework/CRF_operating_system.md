## The eight open design questions to be resolved in the drafting session

**1. Axiom set v0.1.**
Thomas's proposal (April 19): God's motivation, God's character, God's self-imposed limitations, holiness, love, the nature of reality as conscious. Claude's counter-proposal: seven axioms mirroring the Seven Foundational Perspectives (`RM_bootup.md` §7). The two are not incompatible; the Seven Perspectives could be reorganized around Thomas's categories or vice versa. Debate and settle before any derivation is attempted.

**2. Theorem form.**
Confirm operationally what a CRF "theorem" is — the derivation structure that makes explicit what the axioms require, what they prohibit, and what the space of faithful responses is. Define the format: what sections a theorem file carries, what claims are load-bearing, what review standard applies.

**3. Defense document template.**
Design the per-axiom defense artifact. Length, structure, what sources it must cite (Scripture, tradition, philosophy, CPP where applicable), how it gets reviewed, and how a defense interacts with its axiom (attached? separate file? cross-referenced registry?).

**4. Phronesis annotation.**
Every derivation must explicitly acknowledge phronesis as its completion. Decide the structural form: a "phronesis notes" section within each derivation? A separate companion file? A standard closing section of every theorem that names what the formal structure cannot supply?

**5. Nomenclature.**
Whether to adopt CPP's nomenclature directly (AXIM, THEO, PROP, CORL, CONJ, OPEN, PRED-C/O/Q, POST-D, FALS-C, PHEN-E/P/V — see CPP's `templates/nomenclature.md`) or adapt. The Greek root of THEO — *theōria*, seeing clearly — carries especially well in a theological register. CONJ and OPEN map cleanly. PRED-C/O/Q and POST-D may need adaptation since ethical derivations don't produce PDG-comparable numbers. Decide term by term.

**6. Relation to the Theological Grammar.**
`templates/Christos_AI_Theological_Grammar_v1.3.md` carries RM's doctrinal core. Clarify whether CRF axioms are drawn from the Grammar's positions, operate in parallel, or carry a different authority. Whether a CRF theorem can modify a Grammar position, and if so, through what review.

**7. Review protocol.**
CPP uses multi-AI review (Opus primary, ChatGPT, Copilot, Grok historically, Sonnet for hostile review). What review does CRF use? Multi-AI? Fellowship review (the Sunday Zoom)? Both? Does a derivation require theological review by a named human reviewer before it is registered as a theorem?

**8. Folder structure.**
Whether to mirror CPP's evolved pattern (root-level `axiom-registry.md`, `theorem-registry.md`, `Research_Frontier.md`, with `templates/` and `series_*` subfolders) or adapt. CRF may not have "series" in the CPP sense — derivations may be organized by topic (political theology, personal ethics, interfaith engagement, economic ethics) rather than by discovery sequence.

## A note to the session drafting this operating system

Do not simply port the CPP operating system into CRF with ethical terms substituted. The three design commitments in `CRF-README.md` are the *soft-lensing* Thomas named in the April 19 session — they bend the physics-to-ethics analogy appropriately rather than transmitting it literally.

The most common failure mode will be overclaim: treating ethical derivations as if they produce CPP-style unique answers. Watch for it. If the draft operating system ever implies that a CRF theorem produces a single correct action independent of situation and phronesis, it has drifted.
