# Christos Rigorous Framework (CRF)

**Status:** Newborn sub-project, established April 23, 2026.
**Purpose:** The axiom-to-theorem / axiom-to-derivation-structure sub-project of Renaissance Ministries. CRF works the CPP-style rigor in the opposite direction: given the conscious ground of being that CPP establishes physically, what follows for how we should live, govern, love, relate? Axioms are theological and philosophical; theorems are *derivation structures* for right action, not unique-answer deductions.

**Working thesis (Thomas, April 19, 2026):**

> *"The /RM project, in its rigorous expression, is a theological/theoretical proof of the correctness of a given action ... the theorem of right action in any situation."*

Everything CRF does is a working-out of this sentence.

## Three design commitments (from the April 19 convergence)

These are not provisional. They are the conditions on which CRF was agreed to exist, and every later design decision inherits them.

**1. Theorems are derivation structures, not unique-answer deductions.**
Ethics is not physics. CPP's theorems have an external arbiter — particle accelerators confirm or refute the predicted mass. Ethical theorems cannot be tested that way. A CRF theorem makes explicit what the axioms *require*, what they *prohibit*, and what the space of *faithful responses* is. It does not produce 0.02%-error single answers. The discipline is tighter than a sermon and looser than physics.

**2. Every axiom carries a defense document.**
The axioms about God's nature cannot be defended by the system that uses them. In CPP the axioms are defended empirically by the predictions they yield; in CRF they must be defended theologically — by Scripture, tradition, philosophical argument, CPP where applicable. Every axiom registered in CRF will have an attached defense artifact arguing for it from multiple sources. Defenses are part of the corpus, not prerequisites hidden from view.

**3. Phronesis is the formal completion of any derivation.**
CRF generates principles, constraints, and structured derivations. Particular moral action always requires practical wisdom — the guidance of the Holy Spirit in the situation, virtue as a habituated disposition, the fit of a general principle to this person here now. This is explicit from the outset not as a failure of the formal system but as its *proper completion*. Every derivation carries phronesis-notes that name what the formal structure cannot supply.

## Relation to CPP

CPP grounds ontologically from the bottom up: physics points to a conscious ground of being. CRF derives ethically from the top down: given the conscious ground, what follows for action? The two projects are one project seen from opposite ends. Thomas's framing, from the April 19 session: "CPP and RM are one project seen from two ends." This sub-project is where the architectural expression of that claim lives.

CPP may be cited in CRF axioms and derivations at the ontological-ground layer (Level 4 in the authors_voice.md register) — the floor under the comparative moral claim. CRF is not, however, a CPP paper in ethical dress. The methods differ (see design commitment 1 above).
